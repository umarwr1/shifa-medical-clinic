Child Health

Our Medical Clinic offers comprehensive primary care services for children from birth to adolescence. Outpatient appointments are available for infants, children and adolescents up to 18 years of age. Their health and wellbeing are our major priorities.

Why choose Dundas Medical Center for your child?

  • We are committed to improving the health of children.
  • We provide essential behavioral and mental health services on-site.
  • Preventive care including immunizations, health and sports physicals and development checks.
  • Acute visits for when your child is ill.
  • 24/7 hour coverage through the PCN After-Hours clinic — just call (811).
  • Breastfeeding / lactation advice and nutritional advice.
  • PCN mental health services, including consultations with a behavioral health consultant.

Injections

Immunization is a way of providing protection against diseases caused by viruses or bacteria. A vaccine is a substance that stimulates the immune system to produce antibodies against a specific disease without getting the actual disease. Immunization helps prevent serious diseases and the outcomes of diseases.

Our doctors can give intramuscular and subcutaneous injections for the following reasons and more:

  • Immunization
  • Allergy Injections
  • Flu Vaccines
  • Injectable Medications for Medical Reasons
  • Vaccines for Travel

Woman's Health

We provide quality health care services for women in Alberta. We are dedicated to the well-being of all women in the communities we serve. Our staff offers information, support, counselling, and treatment to help women prevent health problems and understand their choices when problems occur.

We provide a broad range of expertise to meet the needs of women with complex conditions including endometriosis and uterine fibroids. Our services:

  • Low Risk Prenatal Care
  • IUD Insertion and Removal
  • Endometrial Biopsies
  • Cervical Screening (Pap Test)
  • Regular Breast Examination
  • Fertility Issues
  • Birth Control Counselling
  • HPV Vaccination
  • Menstrual Concerns
  • Menopausal Issues
  • STI Testing

Lacerations & Cuts

A cut or break in the skin is called a laceration. The cut may be near the surface of the skin or deeper. It can also be smooth or jagged depending on its cause. Based on the depth of the laceration, it can affect the muscles, tendons, ligaments, nerves, or blood vessels.

If your laceration is bleeding profusely, you may need to go to the emergency room. For dressing, suturing, and wound care, please visit our clinic.

Geriatrics

Dundas Medical Center in collaboration with the PCN team can provide Geriatric Medicine Services with specialized assessment and medical care for older adults with age-related disorders including but not limited to the following conditions:

  • Neurocognitive Disorders, Memory Concerns, Dementia
  • Mobility Changes, Gait Disorders, Falls
  • Complex Geriatric Problems, Multiple ER Visits, Polypharmacy
  • Complete Health Assessments

The PCN Team provides geriatric care with goals of increasing functional ability, independence, and quality of life for seniors and their care partners. Treatment is provided by a multidisciplinary team comprising Care of the Elderly Physicians, Nurse Practitioner, Social Worker, Physiotherapist and Occupational Therapist.
